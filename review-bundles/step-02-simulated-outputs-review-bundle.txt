Example 1: 大语言模型

- Mainline: architecture / Transformer -> scaling and few-shot behavior -> data-compute balance.
- Papers:
  1. Attention is All you Need
  2. Language Models are Few-Shot Learners
  3. Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models

8. Self-assessment of output quality
------------------------------------

What works well:

- The default structure makes mode selection, recommendation logic, and evidence disclosure visible.
- The LLM example forms a coherent learning path rather than a citation-count list.
- The AI Agent example clearly avoids saying the three papers are "公认经典".
- The 小红书运营 example respects the user's practical intent while offering better academic reformulations.
- "信息依据" makes anti-hallucination behavior visible in actual output.

What feels too long:

- The Mode A and Mode C examples are lengthy for casual Xiaohongshu consumption.
- Full author lists and evidence notes add friction, but they are useful for trust.
- The current format is more appropriate as a full answer than as a social/card post.

Where hallucination risk remains:

- "Why recommend" sections still contain expert judgment and should not be framed as definitive.
- If future outputs discuss experimental details, they must verify the paper text more deeply.
- AI Agent is evolving quickly, so representative-paper choices may need periodic updates.
- Using "等" for long author lists improves readability but means the output is not a complete citation record.

Whether a future short Xiaohongshu mode may be useful:

- Yes. The full mode is useful for serious learning, but a shorter Xiaohongshu/card mode would likely help scanning and sharing.
- A future short mode could keep:
  - field judgment;
  - 3-paper reading order;
  - one-sentence reason per paper;
  - compact source/evidence note;
  - one next action.
